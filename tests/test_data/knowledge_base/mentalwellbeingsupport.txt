## What supports do we offer?
Whether it’s stress, anxiety, relationships, money worries or workplace pressure—we’re here to look after you always. The 24/7 Mental Wellbeing Support Programme is a confidential, around-the-clock service, provided at no extra cost to all laya healthcare members aged 16+. We offer a wide range of services including:
  - Immediate phone support from Irish-based, qualified psychotherapists
  - Up to 6 face-to-face counselling sessions per issue, per year where necessary
  - Advice on legal, financial, work-related, and personal challenges
  - Faster referrals with counselling sessions offered within 5 working days
  - Unlimited 24/7 access—call as often as you need, anytime
With fast access to our wide range of online and in person mental wellbeing support, at laya health we'll never leave you waiting.

## Looking after your mental health
For many people, finding head space starts with small, everyday moments a walk outdoors in nature, a conversation with a friend, or time to reset as highlighted by laya healthcare brand ambassador Johnny Sexton. But sometimes that isn’t enough. Laya healthcare provides members with access to a 24/7 Mental Wellbeing Support service, offering confidential counselling and professional mental health support at any time of the day or night. Our goal is to make support accessible, reduce stigma around seeking help, and ensure our members always have someone to turn to. Because looking after your mental wellbeing is just as important as looking after your physical health.

## Mind Health Report 2025
As part of the AXA family, we're proud to share the Mind Health Report 2025, offering insights into mental health trends across 16 countries, including Ireland. This year’s report focuses on the evolving challenges faced by young people and the workforce, highlighting how individuals and organisations are responding to mind health needs in a changing world. The report looks at:
  - Mental health awareness, communication, and stigma in workplaces and among young people
  - Global approaches to mind health, from lifestyle habits to digital tools like AI-powered apps
  - Key challenges and emerging trends in mental wellbeing across different cultures and regions
